Singapore Sustainability Dashboard

Project Scope

We had to create an interactive dashboard that delves deep into the sustainability efforts of Singapore.
Through this project, I answered questions through the use of government data available on the internet and showcased my skills in data visualisations, manipulation, analysis and visualisation.

Work Process

We made use of the Crisp-DM method to plan our work process

Requirements Gathering

Based on the national statement (Singapore | Department of Economic and Social Affairs), it seems that Singapore is committed in implementing a sustainable future. Is this true?

Using the above as the main hypothesis, we set out to determine the question: "What is a sustainable future?" Below are the findings:

  • Environmental Sustainability
    • This entails ensuring we mitigate the impacts of climate change
  • Social Sustainability
    • This emphasises creating and maintaining equitable, inclusive, and healthy communities. It involves promoting social cohesion, justice, and well-being for present and future generations.
  • Economic Sustainability
    • This emphasises creating and maintaining the economy that meet the current needs without compromising the ability of future generations to meet their own needs.
Given the above, we set out to investigate 4 different pillars: Urban Planning, Food Security, Renewable Energy and Waste Management.

Modelling

During the data extraction phase, the primary objective is to gather relevant information from identified sources. This involves retrieving data from the selected repositories such as the Department of Singapore Statistics, Energy Market Authority, Building Construction Authority, National Environmental Agency, and Singapore Food Agency. The extraction process is meticulous, ensuring that the collected data is comprehensive and representative of the variables essential for the analysis. Statistical information is gathered to provide a clear overview of the situation, laying the groundwork for subsequent stages in the analytical process.

Evaluation

In the modelling phase, the focus shifts towards constructing a framework or algorithm that can effectively analyze the extracted data. This involves selecting appropriate data models, statistical methods, and machine learning algorithms based on the nature of the problem and the characteristics of the dataset. The goal is to create a robust and accurate model that can unveil patterns, relationships, or trends within the data. This phase may also include data transformation, feature engineering, and other techniques to enhance the model's performance and relevance to the problem at hand.
